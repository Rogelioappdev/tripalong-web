# TripAlong — Monetization Strategy
*Core mechanic: swipe → find trip → want to connect → upgrade*

---

## The Core Conversion Moment

Free users don't upgrade because they ran out of swipes randomly.
They upgrade because they found a trip they love and hit a wall right when they want to act on it.

The limit must fire at **peak intent** — when the user has something to lose, not before they've found anything worth caring about.

```
Swipe feed (free, generous)
    ↓
Find a great trip
    ↓
Want to message someone / join / see who's going
    ↓  ← THIS is where the wall appears
Upgrade prompt
```

---

## Phase 1 — Launch (now → 10k MAU)

### Free tier: 30 swipes/day

Soft. Generous. The goal is habit formation, not pressure.

30 swipes is a real browsing session — 10 to 15 minutes of genuine exploration. Most casual users won't hit it. Power users (the ones eager to find their next trip) will hit it naturally within one or two sessions. That's exactly who you want converting first.

**First session ever: 50 swipes** — give new users a full taste before the daily limit kicks in. They need to see enough trips to get hooked. After Day 1, it drops to 30.

**What happens when they hit 0:**
Don't show a blank screen. Show the next trip card blurred with a soft overlay:
> *"You've seen all your trips for today — come back tomorrow or go unlimited with Plus"*

The card is still visible underneath. They can see there's more. They just can't get to it today. Soft, not aggressive.

**Daily reset notification (opt-in):**
> *"🌍 Your 30 swipes just refreshed — new trips are waiting"*

This creates the daily return habit. The goal in Phase 1 is that users open TripAlong every day. Conversion follows habit.

---

## Phase 2 — Growth (10k → 50k MAU)

### Free tier: 15 swipes/day

Now you tighten. At 10k users the trip card pool is large enough that 15 swipes genuinely leaves users wanting more. Power users hit this in one focused session.

**Introduce the "almost out" nudge:**
At 3 swipes remaining, a soft banner appears:
> *"3 swipes left today"*

Not scary. Just awareness. At 1 swipe left:
> *"Last one for today 👀"*

After 0 — same blurred card as Phase 1.

**Why 15 and not 10:**
10 feels punishing before there's brand trust. 15 feels like a real session that ends at a natural point. You earn the right to go harder as the product matures and users are already invested.

---

## Phase 3 — Scale (50k+ MAU)

### Free tier: 10 swipes/day

Standard mature swipe app limit. At this scale the feed is rich enough that 10 swipes creates real daily urgency. Daily habit is already established from Phase 2. Now the limit works as a proper conversion engine.

## The Empty State — Most Important UX in the App

When a free user hits 0 swipes, this screen is the most important conversion moment in the entire product. Get it right.

**What NOT to do:**
- Hard lock screen with a price and nothing else
- "You've reached your daily limit" (negative framing)
- Hiding the next card completely

**What TO do:**
Show the next trip card, slightly blurred, with:
- The destination still readable
- A soft gradient overlay (not a hard block)
- One line of copy: *"Tokyo is waiting — go unlimited with Plus"*
- Two buttons: **"Get Plus — $7.99/mo"** and *"Come back tomorrow"*

The "come back tomorrow" button is important. It lets users stay without feeling forced. Users who tap it come back tomorrow and form the habit. Users who are eager enough — they convert now.

---

## The Upgrade Prompt — Copy That Converts

Never frame the limit as a punishment. Always frame it as the user being in demand or finding something worth acting on.

| Situation | Copy |
|---|---|
| Hit swipe limit | *"Tokyo is waiting — go unlimited"* |
| Hit conversation cap | *"You've got 5 great connections — upgrade to keep them all going"* |
| Try to see who wants to join | *"5 travelers chose your Bali trip — see who they are"* |
| Try to use advanced filter | *"Filter for trips that actually match your pace and budget"* |
| Try to rewind | *"Want that trip back? Upgrade to rewind"* |

---

## The Habit Loop (Free Users Come Back Every Day)

```
Day 1: 50 swipes (first session) → gets hooked → hits limit
Day 2: "Your 30 swipes refreshed" notification → opens app → swipes → finds great trip
Day 3–7: Same loop → daily habit formed
Day 7–14: Hits limit mid-session on a trip they love → converts to Plus
```

The daily reset notification is not spam — it's the trigger that makes the habit. Users who open TripAlong daily for 7 days before converting have much lower churn than users who convert on Day 1.
