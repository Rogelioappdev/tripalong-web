# TripAlong — Launch Monetization Phasing Plan
*When to introduce limits, what limits, and why*

---

## The Core Problem with Swipe Caps at Small Scale

Before setting any number, understand what you're actually capping.

TripAlong's feed is **trip cards**, not people. If you have 3–5k users and ~15% have posted trips, that's 450–750 active trip cards in the feed at any time. A motivated user can swipe through the entire card pool in one session regardless of a daily cap. The real constraint at launch is **content volume**, not swipe limits.

Tinder caps at 50 swipes/12hr because there are millions of profiles — the cap creates urgency before the user loses interest. You cannot replicate this mechanic without the content volume to back it up. A cap at small scale just hits users before they find anyone good, which causes churn, not upgrades.

---

## Phase-by-Phase Swipe Strategy

### Phase 1 — Launch (Day 0 → 10k MAU or 6 months, whichever comes first)
**Free tier: Unlimited swipes**

No cap. No limit. Full feed access for everyone.

**Why:**
- 450–750 trip cards in the pool — users exhaust the feed naturally, a cap adds nothing
- Network effects require mass participation; any friction at this stage kills discovery
- You need users swiping, joining trips, and chatting to generate the social proof that attracts the next wave of users
- Roammate is free with no cap — you need to match this to compete for early adopters
- Every early user is a potential evangelist. Don't monetize evangelists; grow with them.

**What you monetize in Phase 1 instead:**
The paywall trigger in Phase 1 is NOT swipes — it's **seeing who swiped on your trip**. Free users see "7 travelers want to join your Bali trip" (the count). Plus users see who they are. This creates conversion pressure without touching the swipe feed.

**Duration trigger:** Move to Phase 2 when you hit **10,000 MAU** OR **6 months post-launch**, whichever is first.

---

### Phase 2 — Early Growth (10k MAU → 50k MAU)
**Free tier: 30 swipes/day**

Introduce a generous soft cap. 30/day is enough for a real session — most casual users never hit it — but power users (the people most likely to pay) will feel it.

**Why 30 and not 15:**
- Trip cards are not people cards. Users swipe slower on trips (they read dates, vibes, group size) than on dating profiles. 30 trips/day = a solid 10–15 min session.
- At 10k MAU with ~15% trip creation = ~1,500 trip cards. 30/day means a user sees the full pool in 50 days — natural recycling happens anyway.
- 30 feels generous (not a cash grab) while still hitting the top 20% of users who swipe aggressively.

**What changes at Phase 2:**
- Introduce the swipe cap notification: *"You've seen all trips for today — come back tomorrow or go unlimited with Plus"*
- Pair with the "see who liked you" reveal (primary trigger, already active from Phase 1)
- Launch Trip Boost and Super Swipe consumables here — the first users willing to spend

**Duration trigger:** Move to Phase 3 when you hit **50,000 MAU**.

---

### Phase 3 — Scale (50k MAU → 100k MAU)
**Free tier: 15 swipes/day**

Now the feed has content volume (thousands of trip cards). 15/day creates real, daily recurring pressure. This is the Tinder-equivalent mechanic, but only works because the content pool is large enough that users genuinely want more.

**What changes at Phase 3:**
- Daily swipe refill notification: *"Your 15 swipes just refreshed"* — creates a daily re-engagement habit
- Introduce weekly subscription option if data shows users want short-term access
- Tighten "see who liked you" paywall — higher-intent users at this scale

**Duration trigger:** Move to Phase 4 when you hit **100,000 MAU**.

---

### Phase 4 — Maturity (100k+ MAU)
**Free tier: 10 swipes/day**

Standard niche app free tier. Enough to keep casual users engaged, not enough for power users. Now the cap is the primary conversion trigger because the feed has depth.

## The 6-Month Launch Window — What to Build vs. Wait

### Build at launch (Day 0):
- ✅ "See who liked your trip" count (free) → profile reveal (Plus) — this is the only paywall trigger you need in Phase 1
- ✅ Conversation cap (5 active chats free, unlimited on Plus)
- ✅ Trip Boost consumable ($2.99) — simple, no subscription required, generates first revenue
- ✅ Plus subscription ($7.99/mo or $59.99/yr) with annual as default display

### Wait until Phase 2 (10k MAU):
- ⏳ Super Swipe consumable — needs enough users for the notification to feel meaningful
- ⏳ Pro subscription ($14.99/mo) — not enough power users at launch to justify the tier
- ⏳ Swipe cap — don't introduce until Phase 2

### Wait until Phase 3 (50k MAU):
- ⏳ Advanced analytics / trip insights
- ⏳ AI companion suggestions
- ⏳ Regional pricing
- ⏳ Weekly subscription option

---

## The "Generous Launch Window" Framing

When you introduce limits at Phase 2, frame it as the end of a launch promotion — not a new restriction.

**In-app message when Phase 2 launches:**
> *"During our launch, everyone got unlimited swipes. As TripAlong grows, free accounts now get 30 trips/day — still more than any other travel app. Upgrade to Plus for unlimited."*

This reframes the cap as a natural evolution, not a takeaway. Users who joined during unlimited will feel they got something special, not that something was taken from them.
