## 2. The #1 User Need Every App Gets Wrong

**Compatibility filtering BEFORE the conversation.**

62% of solo travelers plan 2–5 trips/year in 2025 — but they waste hours in chat discovering a budget or pace mismatch. Users report this as their biggest frustration across every app.

| What users want | Who solves it |
|---|---|
| Filter by travel style, budget, pace before matching | Only **Roammate** does this well |
| Verified profiles (not another dating-app-creep experience) | Roammate + GAFFL |
| No misleading paywalls ("free" that isn't) | Roammate only |
| Women-only safety option | Tourlina (but overpriced) |
| Cost-sharing tools | GAFFL (partially) |
| **Trip flake protection** (what if buddy cancels?) | **Nobody solves this** |
| Active users outside major Western cities | Everyone struggles |

**TripAlong's Travel DNA system directly addresses the #1 gap.** Matching on travel style, pace, budget, and preferences before the swipe is the market-leading positioning — lead with this.

## 3. The Two Most Important Competitive Findings

### Finding 1 — Bumble BFF made $0
Bumble's friendship/social mode generated **zero standalone revenue** (Q3 2025) despite meaningful user engagement. Dating-app anxiety triggers (match expiry, blurred "likes") cause **abandonment** in non-romantic contexts, not upgrades. TripAlong must not copy dating-app paywall mechanics directly — design for travel psychology.

### Finding 2 — Roammate is your closest competitor and has a fatal flaw
Roammate is currently the best product in the space (free, verified, deep matching, clean UX). But it is **iOS-only** and has **no monetization**. No monetization = no runway for growth or ads. TripAlong is already on web (Android users covered) and is building a real business model. This is the gap to exploit before Roammate raises money.

## 5. TripAlong Recommended Pricing (Reconciled from Both Research Scans)

### Free Forever — protect network effects at launch
- **Unlimited swipe feed** — never cap at launch (3–10k users). Tinder caps at 50/12hr because they have 75M profiles. You cannot cap at this scale; it will kill discovery. Introduce a soft cap (15/day) only after 10k+ MAU.
- Browse and join public group trip chats
- Basic 1:1 messaging (up to 5 active conversations)
- Create profile + Travel DNA
- Safety verification badge (trust signal — **never paywall this**)
- Post trip plans visible to community

### TripAlong Plus — $7.99/mo or $59.99/yr (~$5/mo)
*Entry tier — accessibility is the priority*
- **See who swiped right on your trip** (count is free; reveal who = Plus)
- Unlimited 1:1 conversations (remove 5-chat cap)
- Advanced matching filters: travel style, budget, pace, accommodation
- Rewind last swipe
- Read receipts

### TripAlong Pro — $14.99/mo or $99.99/yr (~$8.33/mo)
*Power tier — for active trip organizers*
- Everything in Plus
- Create private group trips (free users join public only)
- Profile Boost (1hr/week — appear top of feed)
- Priority placement in swipe feed
- Trip analytics (who viewed, geographic interest breakdown)
- AI companion suggestions

### À-la-carte consumables (revenue from the non-subscribing majority)
- **Trip Boost** — $2.99 · 24-hour priority placement
- **Super Swipe** — $0.99 each · 1 free/day · priority notification to recipient

## 6. Paywall Trigger Priority Order

1. **"X travelers want to travel with you"** → show count free, blur profiles. Reveal behind Plus. *Joy of discovery, not anxiety.*
2. **Conversation cap** → 5 free chats. Hits power users at peak engagement. Upgrade prompt fires naturally.
3. **Private trip creation** → organizers are the network's most valuable users. Pro gating motivates the right people.
4. **Advanced filters** → free gets basic distance/destination. Plus unlocks the compatibility filters (budget, pace, style) that make TripAlong different from every other app.

## 8. Pricing Psychology Rules

- Present annual as **"2 months free"** — outperforms percentage discounts; 60%+ choose annual with right framing
- Keep monthly entry under $10 barrier ($7.99)
- Annual Pro under $100 barrier ($99.99)
- **No weekly plans** — travel planning is monthly/quarterly, not daily compulsive use
- **No free trial at launch** — 55% of trial cancellations happen Day 0 (RevenueCat). Instead offer a genuine free tier with real value
- Add consumables for the majority who won't subscribe
- Anchor UI on annual pricing; show monthly as secondary
- Consider under-25 discount (20%) — solo travelers skew young
